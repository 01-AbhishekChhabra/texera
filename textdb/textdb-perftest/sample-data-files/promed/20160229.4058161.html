27 Feb 2016 Australia: Zika virus: Queensland health authorities spraying at 2nd site
http://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/queensland/zika-virus-queensland-health-authorities-spraying-at-second-site-20160227-gn55wf.html
[Queensland Health authorities are undertaking a 2nd mosquito control response, after a Townsville resident returned to North Queensland with symptoms of Zika. While all cases in Queensland -- with this being the 9th case -- have been imported, authorities have begun pest control spraying in areas where a positive case is returned where the mosquito which carries the virus, _ Aedes aegypti_, is also present.]

24 Feb 2016 China: Health authority warns about Zika virus spreading as weather warms
http://www.cnbc.com/2016/02/24/chinese-authorities-issue-warning-about-zika-virus.html
[China's health authority has warned about the spread of the Zika virus as the weather warms up in mosquito-prone parts of the country.
State news agency Xinhua reported that National Health and Family Planning Commission spokesperson Xiong Huang told a press conference on [Wed 24 Feb 2016] that China had confirmed 5 imported cases of the virus since its 1st case on 9 Feb 2016. The Chinese official said that the country's latest confirmed cases were a 38-year-old man and his 8-year-old son from Yiwu City in Zhejiang Province, who were bitten by mosquitoes while visiting Fiji and Samoa. The other 3 people diagnosed with Zika were from Jiangxi, Guangdong and Zhejiang provinces.]

27 Feb 2017 France: Europe's 1st case of sexually transmitted Zika virus is diagnosed in France as a woman falls ill after her partner travelled to South America
http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-3467037/First-case-sexually-transmitted-Zika-virus-diagnosed-France-woman-falls-ill-partner-travelled-South-America.html
[Woman infected in France when her partner returned from Brazil. Couple lives in Paris and the woman, who is not pregnant, is 'doing well'. Sexual transmission of Zika virus is considered rare, but US health officials are investigating 14 of those cases in the USA, including 2 pregnant women whose infections have been confirmed.]

28 Feb 2016 Honduras: Pregnant woman has Guillain-Barre, possibly Zika
http://abcnews.go.com/Health/wireStory/honduras-pregnant-woman-guillain-barre-possibly-zika-37267011
[Health officials in Honduras say a 29-year-old pregnant woman showing symptoms of the Zika virus has been diagnosed with Guillain-Barre syndrome, a rare disorder that causes paralysis. Authorities say it could be the 1st case in the Central American nation with both conditions. University Hospital spokesman Miguel Osorio says officials are waiting for laboratory tests in the USA to determine whether the woman has been infected by Zika.]

http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/77183359/vaccine-for-zika-virus-may-be-trialled-in-christchurch-as-new-zealand-cases-climb

24 Feb 2016 Pacific: No strong Zika-GBS Link in Pacific Islands
http://tinyurl.com/zxqtlnx
[Past outbreaks of Zika infection did not result in an increase in Guillain-Barre syndrome among children in the Pacific, except in the Solomon Islands in 2015. The increase there from the expected 2 cases to 9 cases was within the confidence limits of statistical variation. See also report above from China. - Mod.JW]

24 Feb 2016 Puerto Rico: freezes condom prices to prevent Zika profiteering
http://www.npr.org/sections/health-shots/2016/02/23/467696092/to-prevent-zika-profiteering-puerto-rico-freezes-condom-prices
[In early February 2016, Puerto Rico's Secretary of the Department of Consumer Affairs Nery Adames Soto announced that his agency has added prophylactics to the price-freeze list. Stores on the island also aren't allowed by DACO [Department of Consumer Affairs] to raise the price on mosquito repellent, window screens, larvicides, and other mosquito-killing products. US CDC data showed that as of 19 Feb 2016 there were 9 cases of Zika reported in Puerto Rico, a US territory; 8 of them were locally acquired and one was associated with travel. Public health officials have reported more than 60 Zika cases in Puerto Rico, including 3 pregnant women.]

26 Feb 2016 USA: CDC details 9 Zika pregnancies: 2 abortions, 2 miscarriages, 1 baby with 'severe microcephaly'
https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/to-your-health/wp/2016/02/26/after-zika-diagnosis-at-least-two-u-s-women-chose-to-have-abortions-cdc-says/
[CDC said that between last August [2015] and 10 Feb 2016, it received more than 257 requests for Zika virus testing of pregnant women in the USA. The vast majority of those cases, 97 percent, tested negative for the disease, but health officials have tracked at least 9 pregnant women in the USA affected with the Zika virus. In addition to the 2 women who had abortions, 2 others suffered miscarriages; 2 are still pregnant. Details of the findings in their fetuses are given.]
